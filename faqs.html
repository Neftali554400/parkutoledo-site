Parking Enforcement & Citations

Parking is enforced at all times — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including nights, weekends, and academic breaks.
You must have a valid permit registered to your license plate. Your plate must face the drive lane. Permits are required at all times including weekends and breaks.
Enforcement officers use license plate readers that scan plates from the drive lane. If your plate is not visible, your permit cannot be verified and you may receive a citation.
You will receive an email notification. You can also log into your vPermit account to check for any outstanding citations.
The issuance date is when the citation was written. The citation date is the date the violation occurred. These are typically the same date.
Pay your citation online through the vPermit portal. Log in and navigate to your citations to make a payment.
Citations must be paid within 14 days of issuance to avoid late fees. If you wish to appeal, you must do so within 14 days.
Late payments will incur additional fees. Unpaid citations may also affect your ability to register for classes or receive university services.
A convenience fee is applied for credit card payments. This fee is charged by the payment processor and is not retained by ParkUToledo.
During a declared snow emergency, parking regulations may be altered. Follow announcements from the University for specific instructions on where to move your vehicle.
"Transferred" means your citation has been sent to the University's student accounts or HR department and will be handled through your tuition or payroll account.
Appeals must be submitted within 14 days of the citation issuance date. Log into the vPermit portal and navigate to your citation to submit an appeal.

The Appeal Process

Yes. You may appeal a citation within 14 days of issuance through the vPermit portal.
Log into your vPermit account, locate the citation, and select "Appeal." Provide your reason and any supporting documentation.
You will receive an email notification once your appeal has been reviewed. You can also check the status in your vPermit account.
Appeals are typically reviewed within 5–10 business days. You will be notified by email of the decision.
You do not need to pay while your appeal is pending. If the appeal is denied, the original fine is due immediately.
Valid grounds include: signage errors, medical emergencies, vehicle malfunction, or proof that a valid permit was purchased but not properly registered.
No. Each citation may only be appealed once. The appeal decision is final.
"Transferred" means the citation has been forwarded to the University's student accounts or payroll office and will be deducted from your account.
You must appeal within 14 days of the citation issuance date. Appeals submitted after this deadline will not be accepted.
