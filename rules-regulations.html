1. Parking Permit Requirements

All vehicles parked on University of Toledo property during enforcement hours must display a valid parking permit or purchase a daily permit through the Parking Portal. Enforcement is active Monday–Friday 7AM–10PM and Saturday–Sunday 9AM–5PM.

Permits are required in all designated parking areas. Signs indicating restricted areas are posted throughout campus. Vehicles without valid permits are subject to citation and/or towing.

2. Purchasing a Parking Permit

Permits may be purchased online through the Parking Portal at vpermit.com/parkutoledo. The following permit types are available:

  • Student Permits: Semester, monthly, and daily. Valid in student-designated zones.
  • Faculty/Staff Permits: Annual and monthly. Payroll deduction available. Valid in employee zones.
  • Visitor/Guest Permits: Daily permits available without account. Starting at $5/day.
  • Contractor Permits: Register at UToledoparking.pmreserve.com.

3. Parking Permit Rules

  • Permits are non-transferable. They may not be shared between individuals or vehicles.
  • Permits must be registered to the vehicle's license plate. A permit linked to one plate does not authorize another vehicle to park.
  • Lost or stolen permits must be reported to the Parking Office immediately.
  • Permits do not guarantee a parking space in any specific lot or area.
  • Permits are valid only in the zone(s) specified at purchase.

4. General Parking Rules

  • Vehicles must be parked within marked stalls at all times.
  • Do not park in fire lanes, on sidewalks, or blocking access to buildings, dumpsters, or loading docks.
  • Accessible parking spaces require a valid state-issued accessible permit or plate in addition to a UT parking permit.
  • No vehicle may be left on campus for longer than 72 hours without prior authorization.
  • Motorcycles and scooters must park in designated motorcycle areas only.

5. Violations and Fines

Common violations and associated fine amounts include but are not limited to:

  • No valid permit displayed — $30
  • Parking in a restricted zone — $40
  • Parking in an accessible space without permit — $250
  • Blocking a fire lane — $75
  • Parking on a sidewalk or grass — $40
  • Overtime parking in a timed area — $25

Fine amounts are subject to change. Refer to the current Rules & Regulations PDF for the most up-to-date schedule.

6. Towing

ParkUToledo reserves the right to tow any vehicle that:

  • Is parked in a fire lane or blocking emergency access.
  • Is abandoned on campus for more than 72 hours.
  • Has been immobilized (booted) and remains in violation.
  • Is parked in a reserved or restricted space without authorization.

Towing charges are the responsibility of the vehicle owner and must be paid to the towing company directly. ParkUToledo is not responsible for towing fees or damages incurred during towing.

7. Immobilization / Booting

Vehicles with three (3) or more unpaid parking citations are subject to immobilization (booting). Once booted, the vehicle cannot be driven until all outstanding fines plus a boot removal fee have been paid in full through the Parking Portal.

If a boot remains on a vehicle for more than 24 hours without payment, the vehicle may be towed at the owner's expense.

8. Collections

Unpaid citations that are not paid or appealed within the required timeframe may be sent to a collections agency. For UT students and employees, unpaid fines may be assessed to your university account, which can result in:

  • A hold on class registration or transcript release.
  • Inability to graduate or receive a diploma.
  • Payroll deduction for employees.

9. Appeals

Citation appeals must be submitted within 10 calendar days of the citation issue date. To file an appeal:

  • Log in to the Parking Portal at vpermit.com/parkutoledo.
  • Navigate to "My Citations" and select "File Appeal."
  • Provide a written explanation and any supporting documentation.

All appeal decisions are made by the ParkUToledo Appeals Committee and are final. Late appeals will not be accepted.

10. Special Event Parking

During university events (athletic events, commencement, etc.), special parking restrictions may be in effect. Affected areas will be posted on campus signage and announced on the ParkUToledo website.

Department event parking requests must be submitted at least 72 hours in advance. See the Department Event Parking page for details.
